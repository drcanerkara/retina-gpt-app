# KONJENİTAL / ERKEN BAŞLANGIÇLI LEZYONLAR

# DISEASE: TORPEDO MACULOPATHY
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Congenital, typically asymptomatic, flat hypopigmented torpedo-shaped lesion temporal to the fovea, often with a pointed “tail” oriented toward the fovea.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: solitary, flat, well-demarcated hypopigmented/depigmented lesion temporal to fovea; torpedo/ovoid with a tapered edge.
- OCT: outer retinal attenuation ± outer retinal cavitation/focal excavation; RPE irregularity; **no** dome-shaped vitelliform subretinal deposit.
- FAF: often mixed hypo/hyper AF corresponding to RPE alteration.
- FA/ICGA: usually minimal non-specific changes; no tumor-type intrinsic vascularity.
- OCTA: usually no neovascular network unless complicated.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs Best/AOFVD: torpedo is typically **flat** and **lacks subretinal hyperreflective vitelliform material** on OCT.
- vs CHRPE: torpedo is usually hypopigmented; CHRPE is typically uniformly hyperpigmented (often with lacunae).
- vs Choroidal nevus: torpedo lacks choroidal mass effect and “tumor” features.

## HIGH-YIELD_PITFALLS
- Do not call it a “mass” unless OCT shows a discrete solid lesion or choroidal thickening.

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- OCT + FAF usually sufficient; monitor if symptomatic or if CNV suspected.

## COMPLICATIONS
- Rare CNV; focal atrophy progression.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- Observation; treat CNV if present.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- Subretinal hemorrhage/exudation, definite choroidal thickening, or a dome-shaped lesion.

# DISEASE: CHRPE (CONGENITAL HYPERTROPHY OF THE RPE)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Flat, sharply demarcated hyperpigmented RPE lesion, sometimes with depigmented lacunae.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: uniformly dark lesion with sharp borders; lacunae may be present.
- OCT: RPE thickening/alteration with shadowing; overlying retina often relatively preserved.
- FAF: generally hypoAF; lacunae may vary.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs Nevus: nevus may have less sharp borders, drusen, subtle elevation; choroidal features.
- vs Torpedo: torpedo is typically hypopigmented and macular-temporal with torpedo contour.

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- Document size/location; consider systemic association only in specific patterns (multiple bilateral lesions).

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- Observation.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- Clear elevation, subretinal fluid, orange pigment, growth → consider melanoma pathway.

# DISEASE: COMBINED HAMARTOMA OF THE RPE AND RETINA
## CORE_PHENOTYPE
- Congenital lesion with surface gliosis and retinal distortion.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: gray elevated lesion with vascular dragging.
- OCT: thickened disorganized retina; ERM-like surface.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs Epiretinal membrane: hamartoma has intrinsic retinal mass component.
- vs CHRPE: CHRPE flat and sharply pigmented.

## MANAGEMENT
- Observation; surgery in select traction cases.

# HEREDİTER / KALITSAL MAKÜLA DİSTROFİLERİ

# DISEASE: BEST DISEASE (BEST1) / VITELLIFORM MACULAR DYSTROPHY
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Inherited vitelliform macular dystrophy with central yellow/orange subretinal deposit and characteristic evolution through stages.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: central (foveal/perifoveal) yellow-orange “egg-yolk” lesion; may fragment (“scrambled egg”) and later become atrophic.
- OCT: **subretinal hyperreflective vitelliform material overlying the RPE** with dome-shaped neurosensory elevation; EZ disruption variable; later RPE/outer retinal atrophy.
- FAF: intense hyperautofluorescence over vitelliform material; later mixed hypo/hyper with atrophy.
- FA/ICGA: usually blocking/hypofluorescence early; late staining depending on stage; evaluate for CNV if suspicious.
- OCTA: CNV screening when symptomatic or exudative signs.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs Stargardt: Stargardt often shows flecks + peripapillary sparing on FAF; Best favors vitelliform mound and **EOG abnormality**.
- vs AOFVD: AOFVD later onset (often middle age), typically smaller lesion; Best often earlier onset; genetics/EOG help.
- vs Torpedo: Best is typically **central** with dome-shaped vitelliform deposit; torpedo is flat temporal lesion without deposit.

## HIGH-YIELD_PITFALLS
- Do not label as “tumor.” This is a deposit/outer retinal-RPE disorder.

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- EOG (Arden ratio), family history, BEST1 genetics; OCTA/FA if CNV suspected.

## COMPLICATIONS
- CNV, fibrosis, geographic atrophy-like changes.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- Observation unless CNV; anti-VEGF for CNV.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- Marked vascular leakage, prominent inflammatory signs, or widespread flecks typical of Stargardt.

# DISEASE: ADULT-ONSET FOVEOMACULAR VITELLIFORM DYSTROPHY (AOFVD) / PATTERN DYSTROPHY (VITELLIFORM TYPE)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Later-onset vitelliform lesion with subretinal deposit resembling Best but typically milder and later presentation.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: small-to-moderate central yellow lesion; often bilateral but asymmetric.
- OCT: subretinal hyperreflective material over RPE; mild dome-shaped elevation; less dramatic than classic Best.
- FAF: focal hyperAF.
- FA/ICGA: variable; evaluate for CNV if symptomatic.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs Best: later onset, often better preserved function; EOG often normal/less abnormal; genetics differ.
- vs CSC: CSC shows serous detachment without a dense vitelliform deposit; pachychoroid features may be present.

## HIGH-YIELD_PITFALLS
- Don’t call “CSC” unless pachychoroid features and typical SRF pattern dominate.

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- OCT/FAF; consider genetics if strong family history; OCTA if CNV suspected.

## COMPLICATIONS
- CNV (uncommon), atrophy over time.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- Observe unless CNV.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- Extensive flecks/peripapillary sparing (think Stargardt), strong inflammatory signs.

# DISEASE: STARGARDT DISEASE (ABCA4) / FUNDUS FLAVIMACULATUS
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Inherited macular dystrophy with flecks, progressive central vision loss, and characteristic FAF pattern (often peripapillary sparing).

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: pisciform yellow-white flecks at posterior pole ± midperiphery; macular atrophy in later disease.
- OCT: EZ loss, outer retinal/RPE atrophy; flecks may correspond to hyperreflective changes.
- FAF: speckled hyper/hypoAF; **peripapillary sparing** common (not universal).
- FA: “dark choroid” classically described (variable).
- OCTA: may show reduced choriocapillaris signal in atrophic areas.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs Best/AOFVD: Stargardt is fleck-driven with broader FAF changes; Best/AOFVD is vitelliform mound.
- vs Cone-rod dystrophy: CORD often has more generalized photoreceptor dysfunction and ERG changes.

## HIGH-YIELD_PITFALLS
- Bull’s-eye is a phenotype; always keep toxicity/CORD/pattern dystrophy in differential.

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- Genetics (ABCA4), ERG depending on phenotype, FAF for pattern mapping.

## COMPLICATIONS
- Progressive atrophy; CNV uncommon but possible.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- Counseling, monitoring; low-vision aids; manage complications.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- Hydroxychloroquine exposure; inflammatory signs; unilateral disease (consider masquerades).

# DISEASE: HYDROXYCHLOROQUINE TOXICITY (BULL’S-EYE PHENOTYPE)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Parafoveal/pericentral photoreceptor-RPE toxicity leading to bull’s-eye maculopathy phenotype.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: may be subtle early; later bull’s-eye RPE change.
- OCT: parafoveal EZ loss (“flying saucer” sign); outer retinal thinning.
- FAF: parafoveal hyperAF ring with central sparing early; later hypoAF atrophy.
- Visual field: paracentral scotomas; mfERG abnormalities.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs Stargardt: exposure history is key; Stargardt has flecks and genetic context.
- vs Cone-rod dystrophy: ERG patterns + systemic medication history.

## HIGH-YIELD_PITFALLS
- Always ask dose/duration and risk factors.

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- OCT + FAF + 10-2/24-2 VF + mfERG as indicated.

## COMPLICATIONS
- Progressive atrophy even after stopping (late).

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- Stop drug in coordination with prescriber when toxicity suspected; monitor.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- No exposure history + strong inherited pattern → inherited dystrophy.

# İNFLAMATUVAR / BEYAZ NOKTA SENDROMLARI VE ÜVEİTİK DURUMLAR

# DISEASE: MEWDS (MULTIPLE EVANESCENT WHITE DOT SYNDROME)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Acute, usually unilateral photopsias and blurred vision in young myopic women; transient outer retinopathy with multiple small white dots and characteristic foveal granularity.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: may be subtle; multiple tiny white dots; foveal “granularity.”
- OCT: focal disruption of EZ/outer retina (often at ellipsoid zone); mild outer retinal attenuation; minimal/no fluid.
- FAF: multiple hyperautofluorescent spots corresponding to lesions.
- FA: punctate hyperfluorescent wreath-like spots.
- ICGA: numerous hypocyanescent spots often more extensive than clinical exam.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs APMPPE: MEWDS has smaller dots, strong photopsias, wreath-like FA, typically benign/self-limited.
- vs AZOOR: AZOOR has larger persistent scotomas and more extensive outer retinal loss.
- vs MFC: MFC tends to scar and recur; MEWDS typically resolves without scarring.

## HIGH-YIELD_PITFALLS
- Mild optic disc edema can be present; do not overcall neuroretinitis without other signs.

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- OCT + FAF ± FA/ICGA; assess symptoms; exclude infectious uveitis if atypical.

## COMPLICATIONS
- Rare CNV; usually excellent recovery.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- Observation in most; short course steroids only if severe/atypical and after excluding infection.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- Bilateral severe disease, progressive scotoma expansion, prominent vitritis, or chorioretinal scarring.

# DISEASE: APMPPE (ACUTE POSTERIOR MULTIFOCAL PLACOID PIGMENT EPITHELIOPATHY)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Acute placoid inflammatory chorioretinopathy with multiple creamy-white lesions at posterior pole; often post-viral; can be bilateral; risk of CNS vasculitis.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: multiple flat placoid yellow-white lesions at posterior pole.
- OCT: outer retinal/RPE disruption at lesion sites; may show focal SRF.
- FAF: lesions often hypoAF acutely with variable hyperAF borders; evolve to mixed AF.
- FA: early hypofluorescence (block/non-perfusion) with late staining.
- ICGA: more numerous hypocyanescent lesions than FA/fundus.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs Serpiginous choroiditis: serpiginous is chronic/recurrent with geographic progression from peripapillary region.
- vs Multifocal choroiditis: MFC scars and recurs; APMPPE tends to “placoid” acute course.
- vs VKH: VKH has diffuse choroiditis, exudative RD, systemic symptoms.

## HIGH-YIELD_PITFALLS
- Always consider neurologic symptoms (headache, focal deficits); systemic evaluation may be required.

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- FA/ICGA + OCT/FAF; consider neuro evaluation if symptomatic.

## COMPLICATIONS
- CNV; permanent scotomas if fovea involved.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- Steroids often used if fovea threatened or extensive disease; coordinate systemic work-up.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- Progressive serpiginous-like edge extension, marked vitritis, or strong infectious risk factors.

# DISEASE: MULTIFOCAL CHOROIDITIS (MFC) / PUNCTATE INNER CHOROIDOPATHY (PIC)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Inflammatory chorioretinopathy in young myopic women; multiple punched-out lesions (MFC often with vitritis; PIC typically minimal/no vitritis) and high CNV risk.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: yellow-gray spots that evolve to punched-out atrophic scars ± pigment.
- OCT: focal outer retinal/RPE disruption; may see inflammatory nodules; CNV signs common.
- FAF: acute lesions often hyperAF; scars become hypoAF.
- FA: early hypofluorescence with late staining; CNV leakage if present.
- ICGA: multiple hypocyanescent spots.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs MEWDS: MEWDS resolves with minimal scarring; MFC/PIC scars and often recurs.
- vs Ocular histoplasmosis: POHS has peripapillary atrophy + punched-out scars but **no** vitritis.
- vs Serpiginous: serpiginous shows geographic peripapillary expansion pattern.

## HIGH-YIELD_PITFALLS
- CNV can be the main driver of vision loss—look carefully on OCT/OCTA.

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- OCTA/FA for CNV; exclude infection if atypical; assess myopia history.

## COMPLICATIONS
- CNV (common), subretinal fibrosis.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- Anti-VEGF for CNV; immunosuppression/steroids for active inflammation when appropriate.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- Significant anterior chamber reaction, necrotizing retinitis features, systemic infection risk.

# DISEASE: SERPIGINOUS CHOROIDITIS
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Chronic, recurrent, progressive geographic chorioretinal inflammation often starting peripapillary and extending in a serpentine pattern.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: gray-white active edge with adjacent atrophic scars; peripapillary origin common.
- OCT: outer retinal/RPE loss; active edges may show hyperreflective inflammation.
- FAF: active edge often hyperAF; old scars hypoAF.
- FA: early hypofluorescence with late staining at active borders.
- ICGA: hypocyanescent lesions highlighting broader extent.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs APMPPE: APMPPE is acute multifocal placoid; serpiginous is chronic/recurrent with peripapillary geographic expansion.
- vs TB serpiginous-like choroiditis: consider TB risk; may be more multifocal; systemic evaluation essential.
- vs AZOOR: AZOOR primarily outer retinal dysfunction without classic choroidal geographic active borders.

## HIGH-YIELD_PITFALLS
- Rule out infectious mimickers (especially TB) in “serpiginous-like” presentations.

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- FA/ICGA + OCT/FAF; infectious work-up if serpiginous-like (TB/syphilis as clinically indicated).

## COMPLICATIONS
- CNV; extensive macular atrophy.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- Immunosuppression typically required; treat infectious cause if identified.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- Prominent vitritis, multifocal lesions without peripapillary pattern, high TB exposure risk.

# DISEASE: AZOOR (ACUTE ZONAL OCCULT OUTER RETINOPATHY)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Acute photopsias with zonal visual field loss and outer retinal dysfunction; fundus can be initially normal; often young myopic women.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: may be normal early; later zonal RPE changes.
- OCT: zonal EZ/outer retinal loss corresponding to symptoms.
- FAF: zonal hyperAF border with hypoAF/atrophy in involved areas (varies).
- FA: often minimal; used to exclude mimics.
- ERG: may show localized/multifocal dysfunction (mfERG helpful).

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs MEWDS: MEWDS has discrete dots/wreath FA and tends to resolve; AZOOR has persistent zonal defects.
- vs Cone-rod dystrophy: inherited dystrophies are more symmetric/progressive; family history/genetics.
- vs Autoimmune retinopathy: AIR often bilateral with rapid dysfunction and minimal fundus changes.

## HIGH-YIELD_PITFALLS
- Symptoms (photopsias) and field defects may be disproportionate to fundus appearance.

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- OCT + FAF + VF ± mfERG; consider autoimmune/infectious evaluation when appropriate.

## COMPLICATIONS
- Persistent scotomas; progressive atrophy in some.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- Variable; consider immunomodulatory therapy in select progressive cases after work-up.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- Marked inflammation, vitritis, necrotizing lesions, or systemic infection signs.

# DISEASE: VKH (VOGT–KOYANAGI–HARADA DISEASE) — POSTERIOR SEGMENT SIGNATURE
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Bilateral granulomatous panuveitis with diffuse choroiditis and exudative retinal detachment; systemic neurologic/auditory/integumentary symptoms may occur.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: serous/exudative detachments; hyperemic disc in acute phase.
- OCT: multilobular serous RD with septae; choroidal thickening (EDI-OCT).
- FAF: variable; can show widespread RPE disturbance over time.
- FA: multiple pinpoint leaks with pooling (“starry-sky”); disc leakage.
- ICGA: diffuse choroidal hypofluorescent dark dots.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs CSC: CSC lacks inflammatory signs and typically no disc leakage; VKH has choroidal thickening and diffuse leakage.
- vs Posterior scleritis: may have pain and T-sign on ultrasound.
- vs Sympathetic ophthalmia: trauma/surgery history; similar phenotype.

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- Clinical uveitis exam + OCT/FA/ICGA; systemic review; exclude infectious uveitis before high-dose steroids.

## COMPLICATIONS
- Sunset-glow fundus, choroidal atrophy, CNV.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- High-dose corticosteroids + early immunosuppression as indicated; specialist management.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- Unilateral isolated SRF without inflammation; focal leakage pattern typical of CSC.

# DISEASE: CENTRAL SEROUS CHORIORETINOPATHY (CSC)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Serous neurosensory detachment associated with pachychoroid features.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: subtle foveal reflex change; mild RPE alterations.
- OCT: subretinal fluid; PED may be present; pachychoroid signs if enhanced depth.
- FAF: gravitational tracks in chronic disease; mixed AF changes.
- FA: focal “inkblot/smokestack” leakage; chronic diffuse leakage.
- ICGA: choroidal hyperpermeability; pachyvessels.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs Vitelliform: CSC SRF lacks dense subretinal vitelliform hyperreflective deposit.
- vs Type 1 MNV: look for shallow irregular RPE elevation and CNV signs (OCTA/ICGA).

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- OCT ± OCTA; FA/ICGA when atypical or chronic.

## COMPLICATIONS
- Chronic RPE damage, secondary CNV.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- Risk factor modification; PDT in chronic cases; treat CNV if present.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- Hemorrhage/exudation suggest CNV; significant inflammation suggests uveitic causes.

# DISEASE: MACULAR TELANGIECTASIA TYPE 2 (MacTel 2)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Parafoveal neurodegenerative-vascular disorder with characteristic inner/outer retinal changes.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: subtle parafoveal graying; right-angle venules; crystalline deposits may be present.
- OCT: inner/outer retinal cavitations (often non-exudative); ILM drape; EZ loss temporal to fovea.
- FAF: parafoveal increased AF (loss of macular pigment signal pattern).
- FA: late parafoveal leakage (often without cystoid edema).
- OCTA: telangiectatic changes; possible secondary neovascularization.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs DME: MacTel cavities often non-exudative with minimal thickening.
- vs CSC: CSC has SRF and pachychoroid features; MacTel has characteristic parafoveal structural pattern.

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- OCT + FAF + FA/OCTA as needed.

## COMPLICATIONS
- Subretinal neovascularization.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- Observe; treat neovascular complications.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- Marked exudation/thickening (consider DME/vascular).

# VASKÜLER OKLÜZYONLAR VE İSKEMİK RETİNOPATİLER

# DISEASE: CENTRAL RETINAL ARTERY OCCLUSION (CRAO)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Sudden, painless, severe unilateral vision loss with diffuse inner retinal ischemia and classic cherry-red spot.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: retinal whitening (posterior pole), attenuated arteries, cherry-red fovea.
- OCT: hyperreflective thickened inner retina acutely; later inner retinal thinning/atrophy.
- FAF: variable; may show subtle hypoAF corresponding to ischemia.
- FA: delayed/absent arterial filling; prolonged arteriovenous transit.
- OCTA: marked reduction of superficial/deep capillary plexus flow.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs Ophthalmic artery occlusion: more profound ischemia with choroidal filling delay.
- vs Severe CRVO: CRVO shows dilated tortuous veins and hemorrhages.
- vs Compressive optic neuropathy: fundus ischemic whitening absent.

## HIGH-YIELD_PITFALLS
- Cilioretinal artery sparing may preserve central vision.

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- Urgent stroke work-up; carotid imaging; cardiac evaluation; ESR/CRP if GCA suspected.

## COMPLICATIONS
- Neovascularization (iris/angle).

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- Emergency systemic evaluation; manage underlying vascular risk factors.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- Prominent hemorrhages and venous dilation (think CRVO).

# DISEASE: BRANCH RETINAL ARTERY OCCLUSION (BRAO)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Sectoral retinal ischemia corresponding to an embolic occlusion of a branch artery.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: sectoral retinal whitening along distribution of a branch artery.
- OCT: inner retinal hyperreflectivity/thickening in involved territory.
- FA: delayed filling in affected branch.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs Cotton-wool spots: CWS are smaller, superficial nerve fiber infarcts without large sectoral pattern.
- vs CRVO: no venous dilation/hemorrhagic pattern.

## WORK-UP
- Embolic source evaluation (carotid/cardiac).

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW
- Systemic vascular risk management.

# DISEASE: CENTRAL RETINAL VEIN OCCLUSION (CRVO)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Dilated tortuous veins with widespread retinal hemorrhages (“blood and thunder”).

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: diffuse hemorrhages (all quadrants), venous engorgement, disc edema.
- OCT: macular edema common; cystoid spaces; subretinal fluid possible.
- FA: delayed venous filling; ischemic areas; leakage.
- OCTA: capillary non-perfusion zones.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs BRVO: BRVO is sectoral.
- vs OIS: OIS shows mid-peripheral hemorrhages and arterial narrowing with carotid disease.

## HIGH-YIELD_PITFALLS
- Differentiate ischemic vs non-ischemic CRVO (risk of NV).

## WORK-UP
- Assess ischemia; systemic vascular work-up.

## COMPLICATIONS
- Neovascular glaucoma; macular edema.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW
- Anti-VEGF for macular edema; monitor for neovascularization.

# DISEASE: BRANCH RETINAL VEIN OCCLUSION (BRVO)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE
- Sectoral hemorrhages and edema along distribution of occluded branch vein.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: flame/dot hemorrhages in one quadrant; dilated vein.
- OCT: cystoid macular edema if fovea involved.
- FA: sectoral non-perfusion and leakage.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs CRVO: localized vs diffuse involvement.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW
- Anti-VEGF for macular edema; laser in selected ischemic areas.

# DISEASE: OCULAR ISCHEMIC SYNDROME (OIS)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE
- Chronic hypoperfusion due to severe carotid stenosis; gradual vision loss with mid-peripheral hemorrhages.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: narrowed arteries, mid-peripheral dot-blot hemorrhages.
- FA: delayed choroidal filling; prolonged arteriovenous transit.
- OCT: chronic macular edema possible.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs CRVO: OIS has carotid disease and delayed choroidal filling.
- vs Diabetic retinopathy: distribution and systemic carotid findings.

## WORK-UP
- Urgent carotid Doppler; vascular evaluation.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW
- Treat systemic carotid disease; ocular complications as needed.

# DISEASE: RETINAL ARTERIAL MACROANEURYSM
## CORE_PHENOTYPE
- Focal dilation of retinal arteriole in elderly hypertensive patients.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: round red lesion along arteriole; hemorrhage/exudation may surround.
- OCT: intraretinal/subretinal fluid if exudative.
- FA: focal early hyperfluorescence with late leakage.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs PCV: PCV subretinal polypoidal network; macroaneurysm is arterial branch-based.
- vs Nevus/melanoma: vascular lesion with leakage, not pigmented mass.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW
- Observation vs anti-VEGF/laser if vision-threatening exudation.

# YAŞA BAĞLI VE DEJENERATİF MAKÜLA HASTALIKLARI

# DISEASE: AGE-RELATED MACULAR DEGENERATION (AMD) — EARLY / INTERMEDIATE (NON-NEOVASCULAR)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Age-related macular disease characterized by drusen and RPE dysfunction; risk of progression to geographic atrophy (GA) or neovascular AMD.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: soft drusen, pigmentary changes, RPE mottling; may show reticular pseudodrusen.
- OCT: drusen (sub-RPE elevations), RPE irregularity; may show hyperreflective foci; outer retinal attenuation in advanced non-neovascular disease.
- FAF: patchy hyper/hypoAF with RPE stress; reticular patterns may be present.
- FA: usually for CNV suspicion; otherwise limited routine role.
- OCTA: useful if occult CNV suspected despite “dry” appearance.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs Pachychoroid spectrum: AMD often has **drusen**; pachychoroid often has thick choroid/pachyvessels with fewer typical drusen.
- vs Pattern dystrophy/AOFVD: dystrophies often have earlier onset, more symmetric vitelliform deposits, and different FAF signatures.

## HIGH-YIELD_PITFALLS
- Do not assume “dry AMD” if there is subretinal/intraretinal fluid—evaluate for MNV (type 1/2/3 or PCV/PNV).

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- OCT baseline + periodic follow-up; consider OCTA/FA if symptoms or subtle exudation.

## COMPLICATIONS
- Progression to GA or neovascular AMD.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- Risk factor control; AREDS2 supplementation if intermediate AMD as appropriate; monitor for conversion.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- Younger age, absent drusen, strong pachychoroid signs, marked inflammation.

# DISEASE: GEOGRAPHIC ATROPHY (GA) — ADVANCED NON-NEOVASCULAR AMD
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Well-demarcated areas of RPE and outer retinal atrophy with progressive enlargement.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: sharply demarcated atrophic patches with visible choroidal vessels.
- OCT: complete RPE and outer retinal atrophy (outer retinal loss, RPE loss, choroidal hypertransmission); edges may show hyperreflective foci.
- FAF: sharply demarcated hypoAF (atrophy) with variable hyperAF rims indicating active border.
- OCTA: reduced choriocapillaris flow in atrophic zones; useful to assess for coexistent MNV.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs Stargardt: earlier onset, flecks, peripapillary sparing pattern often suggests Stargardt.
- vs Inflammatory scars: inflammatory lesions often have irregular borders and history of uveitis.

## HIGH-YIELD_PITFALLS
- GA and MNV can coexist; fluid/hemorrhage may indicate mixed phenotype (treat MNV).

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- OCT + FAF for progression mapping; OCTA if concern for MNV.

## COMPLICATIONS
- Severe central vision loss; secondary MNV possible.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- Monitor progression; consider emerging GA therapies per local availability; treat MNV if present.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- Marked vitritis/inflammation, unilateral focal scar pattern.

# DISEASE: NEOVASCULAR AMD (nAMD) — TYPE 1/2/3 MNV IN AMD CONTEXT
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Exudative AMD due to macular neovascularization causing fluid, hemorrhage, and vision loss.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: subretinal hemorrhage, lipid, gray-green membrane, PED.
- OCT: intraretinal fluid and/or subretinal fluid; sub-RPE hyperreflective material; PED; subretinal hyperreflective material (SHRM).
- FA: classic/occult leakage depending on type; useful if diagnosis uncertain.
- OCTA: neovascular network identification and monitoring (adjunct).

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs CSC/PNV/PCV: evaluate choroid thickness, pachyvessels, polypoidal features (ICGA helpful).
- vs MacTel neovascular: MacTel has characteristic parafoveal neurodegenerative signs.

## HIGH-YIELD_PITFALLS
- Do not label fluid as “CSC” in older patients with drusen/SHRM—rule out nAMD first.

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- OCT baseline; FA/OCTA/ICGA if atypical or suspected PCV/PNV.

## COMPLICATIONS
- Subretinal fibrosis, atrophy, hemorrhage.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- Anti-VEGF mainstay; treat-and-extend strategies; manage PCV/PNV variants if present.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- Strong inflammatory signs; infectious mimics.

# DISEASE: RETICULAR PSEUDODRUSEN (SUBRETINAL DRUSENOID DEPOSITS)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Subretinal deposits associated with higher risk of GA and progression.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: subtle reticular pattern, often hard to see.
- OCT: subretinal drusenoid deposits above the RPE.
- FAF: reticular hypoAF patterns.
- IR imaging: reticular network more apparent.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs Conventional drusen: conventional drusen are sub-RPE; RPD are subretinal.

## CLINICAL_IMPORTANCE
- Increased risk for GA; close monitoring.

# DİYABETİK RETİNOPATİ VE DİĞER MİKROVASKÜLER HASTALIKLAR

# DISEASE: DIABETIC RETINOPATHY (OVERVIEW)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Chronic microvascular retinal disease due to diabetes mellitus leading to capillary leakage, ischemia, and neovascularization.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: microaneurysms, dot-blot hemorrhages, hard exudates, cotton-wool spots.
- OCT: macular edema (intraretinal cysts, diffuse thickening); may show subretinal fluid.
- FA: capillary non-perfusion; microaneurysm leakage; neovascular leakage in advanced stages.
- OCTA: capillary dropout; enlargement of FAZ.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs Radiation retinopathy: similar microangiopathy but radiation history present.
- vs Hypertensive retinopathy: arteriole narrowing, AV nicking more prominent.

## HIGH-YIELD_PITFALLS
- Macular edema can occur at any stage of DR.

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- Systemic glycemic control assessment; OCT baseline; FA if ischemia unclear.

## COMPLICATIONS
- Diabetic macular edema (DME); proliferative DR; tractional RD.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- Glycemic control; anti-VEGF for DME/PDR; PRP in PDR.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- Unilateral severe findings without systemic explanation.

# DISEASE: NONPROLIFERATIVE DIABETIC RETINOPATHY (NPDR)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE
- Microvascular changes without neovascularization.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Mild NPDR: microaneurysms only.
- Moderate NPDR: hemorrhages, exudates.
- Severe NPDR: 4-2-1 rule (hemorrhages in 4 quadrants, venous beading in 2, IRMA in 1).
- OCT: macular edema may coexist.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs Retinal vein occlusion: RVO more sectoral; DR more symmetric and bilateral.

## MANAGEMENT
- Monitor; treat DME if present.

# DISEASE: DIABETIC MACULAR EDEMA (DME)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE
- Retinal thickening involving macula due to capillary leakage.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- OCT: intraretinal cystoid spaces; diffuse thickening; hyperreflective foci; possible SRF.
- FA: focal or diffuse leakage from microaneurysms.
- OCTA: capillary dropout in chronic cases.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs MacTel: MacTel cavities often non-exudative and parafoveal.
- vs CRVO macular edema: CRVO has widespread hemorrhages and venous dilation.

## MANAGEMENT
- Anti-VEGF first-line; steroids in selected cases.

# DISEASE: PROLIFERATIVE DIABETIC RETINOPATHY (PDR)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE
- Retinal neovascularization due to ischemia.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: neovascularization of disc (NVD) or elsewhere (NVE); preretinal/vitreous hemorrhage.
- FA: extensive capillary non-perfusion; leakage from neovascularization.
- OCT: tractional membranes; vitreomacular traction.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs OIS: OIS shows carotid-related delayed choroidal filling.
- vs Sickle cell retinopathy: peripheral sea-fan neovascularization.

## HIGH-YIELD_PITFALLS
- Small NVE can be subtle; FA helpful if uncertain.

## COMPLICATIONS
- Vitreous hemorrhage; tractional retinal detachment.

## MANAGEMENT
- PRP ± anti-VEGF; vitrectomy if non-clearing hemorrhage or traction.

# DISEASE: TRACTIONAL RETINAL DETACHMENT (DIABETIC CONTEXT)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE
- Retinal detachment due to fibrovascular membrane contraction.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: elevated retina with taut fibrovascular membranes.
- OCT: vitreoretinal traction; elevation of neurosensory retina.
- FA: neovascular leakage in active areas.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs Rhegmatogenous RD: TRD lacks full-thickness break; concave configuration often.

## MANAGEMENT
- Vitrectomy in vision-threatening cases.

# DISEASE: HYPERTENSIVE RETINOPATHY
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Retinal arteriolar changes and ischemic signs related to systemic hypertension.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: generalized arteriolar narrowing, AV nicking, copper/silver wiring; flame hemorrhages; cotton-wool spots in severe cases; disc edema in malignant HTN.
- OCT: may show macular edema in severe cases.
- FA: may show ischemia/leakage if severe.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs Diabetic retinopathy: DR is microaneurysm-driven and often bilateral symmetric; HTN emphasizes arteriolar signs and flame hemorrhages.
- vs RVO: RVO is sectoral (BRVO) or diffuse venous engorgement (CRVO).

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW
- Systemic BP control; ocular complications management as needed.

# DISEASE: RADIATION RETINOPATHY
## CORE_PHENOTYPE
- Delayed microangiopathy after ocular/orbital radiation.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: microaneurysms, hemorrhages, cotton-wool spots.
- OCT: macular edema common.
- FA: capillary non-perfusion.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs Diabetic retinopathy: radiation history key; distribution often posterior.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW
- Anti-VEGF for edema; monitor ischemia.

# DISEASE: HYPERCOAGULABLE / SYSTEMIC COAGULOPATHY-RELATED RETINOPATHY
## CORE_PHENOTYPE
- Retinal vascular occlusions in younger patients with systemic clotting disorders.

## IMAGING
- Similar to CRVO/BRVO/arterial occlusions depending on vessel involved.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- Younger age; recurrent events; systemic history.

## WORK-UP
- Hypercoagulable panel; systemic coordination.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW
- Treat underlying systemic disorder; manage ocular complications.

# PATOLOJİK MİYOPİ VE İLİŞKİLİ KOMPLİKASYONLAR

# DISEASE: PATHOLOGIC MYOPIA (MYOPIC MACULOPATHY OVERVIEW)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Progressive degenerative changes of the posterior pole in high axial myopia associated with posterior staphyloma, chorioretinal atrophy, and risk of myopic MNV.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: tessellated fundus, peripapillary atrophy, lacquer cracks, patchy chorioretinal atrophy.
- OCT: posterior staphyloma curvature; thin choroid; outer retinal and RPE atrophy; myopic tractional changes.
- FAF: hypoAF in atrophic areas; variable patterns in early changes.
- FA: used primarily to detect MNV.
- OCTA: detection of myopic MNV.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs AMD: myopic patients younger; choroid markedly thin; absence of typical soft drusen.
- vs Inflammatory scars: inflammatory scars often irregular and associated with vitritis history.

## HIGH-YIELD_PITFALLS
- Myopic MNV can be subtle and mistaken for inflammatory CNV.

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- Axial length; OCT + OCTA; FA if uncertain.

## COMPLICATIONS
- Myopic MNV; macular schisis; macular hole; retinal detachment.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- Anti-VEGF for MNV; monitor tractional complications.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- Thick choroid with pachyvessels (consider pachychoroid instead).

# DISEASE: MYOPIC MACULAR NEOVASCULARIZATION (MYOPIC MNV)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE
- Small, classic-appearing neovascular membrane in highly myopic eyes.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: small gray subfoveal lesion ± hemorrhage.
- OCT: hyperreflective subretinal lesion; minimal PED; thin choroid.
- FA: classic early hyperfluorescence with leakage.
- OCTA: compact neovascular network.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs nAMD: AMD has drusen and thicker choroid.
- vs Inflammatory CNV (MFC/PIC): inflammatory scars present in MFC/PIC.

## MANAGEMENT
- Anti-VEGF (often fewer injections than AMD).

# DISEASE: MYOPIC TRACTION MACULOPATHY / FOVEOSCHISIS
## CORE_PHENOTYPE
- Splitting of retinal layers due to posterior staphyloma and vitreoretinal traction.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- OCT: schisis cavities in inner/outer retina; ILM detachment; possible macular hole.
- Color fundus: subtle changes; high myopia context.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs DME: no exudation; schisis pattern distinct.
- vs MacTel: MacTel cavitations parafoveal and not staphyloma-driven.

## MANAGEMENT
- Observation vs surgery if progressive.

# DISEASE: LACQUER CRACKS
## CORE_PHENOTYPE
- Breaks in Bruch’s membrane in high myopia.

## IMAGING
- Fundus: fine yellowish linear streaks.
- FA: window defects.
- OCT: subtle Bruch’s membrane irregularity.

## CLINICAL_IMPORTANCE
- Risk marker for myopic MNV.

# VİTREORETİNAL ARAYÜZEY HASTALIKLARI

# DISEASE: EPIRETINAL MEMBRANE (ERM)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Fibrocellular membrane on ILM causing tangential traction, inner retinal wrinkling, and metamorphopsia.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: macular sheen, retinal surface wrinkling; vessel straightening.
- OCT: hyperreflective line on retinal surface; foveal distortion, thickening; ectopic inner foveal layers (EIFL) in advanced cases; ± pseudohole/lamellar changes.
- FA: usually non-specific; may show leakage if associated edema.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs VMT: VMT shows focal vitreous adhesion with traction; ERM is a surface membrane.
- vs Macular hole: ERM causes pseudohole/lamellar configurations without full-thickness defect.

## HIGH-YIELD_PITFALLS
- Do not call “CME” unless intraretinal cysts are present; ERM can cause non-cystic thickening.

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- OCT severity staging (consider EIFL presence); symptoms.

## COMPLICATIONS
- CME, lamellar hole, foveoschisis-like changes (rare).

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- Observation vs vitrectomy with membrane peel when symptomatic.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- Sudden onset with inflammation; significant hemorrhage.

# DISEASE: VITREOMACULAR TRACTION (VMT)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Incomplete PVD with persistent focal vitreous adhesion causing anteroposterior traction and foveal distortion.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- OCT: focal vitreous attachment with tractional elevation; intraretinal cysts may occur; possible impending macular hole.
- Color fundus: often subtle.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs ERM: ERM is a surface membrane; VMT shows vitreous attachment.
- vs Macular hole: VMT may precede FTMH; evaluate for full-thickness defect.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW
- Observe if mild; pharmacologic vitreolysis or surgery in selected cases.

# DISEASE: FULL-THICKNESS MACULAR HOLE (FTMH)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Full-thickness defect at fovea, typically idiopathic due to vitreofoveal traction.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- OCT: full-thickness foveal gap with elevated edges; operculum may be present; assess minimum linear diameter and outer retinal status.
- Color fundus: central reddish spot; may be difficult to see.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs Lamellar hole: lamellar has partial-thickness defect with preserved outer retina.
- vs Pseudohole (ERM): steep foveal contour without full-thickness loss.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW
- Surgical repair (PPV + ILM peel + gas) in symptomatic holes.

# DISEASE: LAMELLAR MACULAR HOLE (LMH) vs PSEUDOHLE
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- LMH: partial-thickness foveal defect with tissue loss; Pseudohole: ERM-related steepened foveal contour without true tissue loss.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- OCT (LMH): irregular foveal contour, intraretinal splitting, partial-thickness defect; possible lamellar hole-associated epiretinal proliferation (LHEP).
- OCT (Pseudohole): steep foveal pit with ERM traction; intact outer retina.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- LMH has tissue loss; pseudohole does not.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW
- Individualized; surgery for symptomatic progressive cases.

# KOROIDAL VE RETİNAL TÜMÖRLER

# DISEASE: CHOROIDAL NEVUS (BASICS)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Benign choroidal melanocytic lesion; usually flat-to-minimally elevated.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: slate-gray lesion, variable borders; drusen may suggest chronicity.
- OCT: may show overlying RPE changes; assess for SRF.
- FAF: drusen/hyperAF spots; orange pigment (lipofuscin) is concerning.
- Ultrasound: thickness assessment if elevated.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs Melanoma: thickness, SRF, symptoms, orange pigment, proximity to disc, ultrasonographic hollowness (risk features).
- vs CHRPE: CHRPE is RPE-level and sharply demarcated.

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- Baseline photos, OCT; ultrasound if needed.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- Observe; refer if risk features.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- Subretinal fluid, orange pigment, documented growth, symptoms.

# DISEASE: CHOROIDAL MELANOMA (BASICS FOR DIFFERENTIAL)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE
- Malignant choroidal melanocytic tumor with risk of metastasis.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: dome or mushroom-shaped elevated pigmented mass.
- OCT: choroidal elevation; subretinal fluid; overlying RPE changes.
- Ultrasound: low-medium internal reflectivity.
- FAF: orange pigment (lipofuscin) hyperAF.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs Nevus: thickness >2 mm, SRF, symptoms, orange pigment, documented growth.
- vs Hemangioma: hemangioma is orange-red and highly vascular.

## MANAGEMENT
- Urgent referral to ocular oncology.

# DISEASE: CIRCUMSCRIBED CHOROIDAL HEMANGIOMA
## CORE_PHENOTYPE
- Benign vascular tumor of choroid.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: orange-red elevated mass.
- OCT: choroidal thickening with SRF.
- FA: early hyperfluorescence with late staining.
- ICGA: early intense hypercyanescence.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs Melanoma: hemangioma more orange, highly vascular, high reflectivity on ultrasound.

## MANAGEMENT
- PDT for symptomatic SRF.

# DISEASE: CHOROIDAL OSTEOMA
## CORE_PHENOTYPE
- Benign ossifying tumor of choroid in young women.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: yellow-white plaque-like lesion with distinct borders.
- OCT: choroidal hyperreflective lamellar structure.
- Ultrasound/CT: calcified lesion (high reflectivity).

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs Amelanotic melanoma: osteoma calcifies and has bone density.
- vs Sclerochoroidal calcification: different location/systemic context.

## MANAGEMENT
- Monitor; treat CNV if develops.

# DISEASE: RETINOBLASTOMA (POSTERIOR SEGMENT SIGNATURE)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE
- Pediatric intraocular tumor with calcification.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: white mass ± seeding.
- OCT: limited role; elevated mass.
- Ultrasound: calcifications.
- CT: calcification diagnostic.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs Coats disease: Coats shows telangiectasia/exudation without calcification.
- vs Persistent fetal vasculature: different anatomy.

## MANAGEMENT
- Urgent oncology referral.

# DISEASE: RETINAL ASTROCYTIC HAMARTOMA
## CORE_PHENOTYPE
- Benign glial tumor often associated with tuberous sclerosis.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: mulberry-like white lesion.
- OCT: hyperreflective intraretinal mass with shadowing.
- FAF: variable.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs Retinoblastoma: astrocytic hamartoma stable and non-aggressive; clinical context critical.

## MANAGEMENT
- Observe; systemic evaluation if multiple.

# DISEASE: CAVERNOUS HEMANGIOMA OF THE RETINA
## CORE_PHENOTYPE
- Benign vascular tumor with grape-like clusters of dilated vessels.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: saccular, dark red clusters.
- FA: slow filling without leakage.
- OCT: superficial vascular clusters.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs Retinoblastoma: no calcification; benign stable lesion.
- vs Coats disease: Coats shows exudation and telangiectasia.

## MANAGEMENT
- Observation.

# DISEASE: CHOROIDAL METASTASIS
## CORE_PHENOTYPE
- Secondary choroidal tumor from systemic malignancy.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: creamy yellow plateau-shaped lesion.
- OCT: choroidal thickening with SRF.
- FA: early hypofluorescence with late staining.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs Melanoma: metastasis often creamy, multifocal, and systemic cancer history present.
- vs Hemangioma: hemangioma intensely vascular on ICGA.

## MANAGEMENT
- Oncology referral.

# DİĞER ÖNEMLİ RETİNA HASTALIKLARI

# DISEASE: ACUTE RETINAL NECROSIS (ARN)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Necrotizing herpetic retinitis with occlusive vasculitis and prominent intraocular inflammation; high risk of retinal detachment.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: peripheral patches of retinal whitening/necrosis progressing circumferentially; arteritis; hemorrhage.
- OCT: limited utility in peripheral disease; macular involvement variable.
- FAF: may show hypoAF corresponding to necrosis.
- FA: occlusive vasculitis, capillary non-perfusion.
- PCR (aqueous/vitreous): HSV/VZV confirmation.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs CMV retinitis: CMV in immunosuppressed; less vitritis; “pizza pie” hemorrhagic necrosis.
- vs Toxoplasmosis: focal retinitis often adjacent to scar; different vasculitis pattern.

## HIGH-YIELD_PITFALLS
- This is an emergency; delays increase RD risk.

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- Immediate aqueous/vitreous PCR; systemic immune status review.

## COMPLICATIONS
- Rhegmatogenous RD, optic neuropathy, severe vision loss.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- Urgent antiviral therapy ± intravitreal antivirals; manage inflammation; prophylaxis/monitor RD.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- Minimal inflammation with classic CMV pattern in immunocompromised.

# DISEASE: OCULAR TOXOPLASMOSIS (POSTERIOR UVEITIS SIGNATURE)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Focal necrotizing retinochoroiditis often adjacent to an old scar; vitritis (“headlight in the fog”).

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: fluffy white focal retinitis with overlying vitreous haze; old pigmented scar may be present.
- OCT: hyperreflective inner retinal lesion with shadowing; adjacent edema; possible SRF.
- FAF: variable; active lesion often hyperAF with surrounding changes.
- FA: early hypofluorescence with late staining; vasculitis can occur.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs ARN: ARN is more peripheral, rapidly progressive, with prominent occlusive arteritis.
- vs Bacterial/fungal endophthalmitis: more diffuse involvement and severe pain/systemic context.

## WORK-UP_/_CONFIRMATORY_TESTS
- Serology supportive; PCR in atypical cases; assess immune status.

## COMPLICATIONS
- CNV, traction, recurrent episodes.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW_(BRIEF)
- Anti-toxo therapy + steroids when appropriate; specialist management.

## RED_FLAGS_(CONSIDER_ALTERNATIVE_IF)
- Very rapid circumferential peripheral necrosis; severe occlusive arteritis (think ARN).

# DISEASE: CMV RETINITIS (SIGNATURE)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Necrotizing retinitis in immunocompromised patients with minimal vitritis and characteristic hemorrhagic pattern.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: granular white retinal necrosis with hemorrhage (“pizza pie”), often along vessels.
- FA: may show vasculitis and non-perfusion.
- OCT: variable; depends on location.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS_(AVOID_CONFUSION_WITH)
- vs ARN: ARN has prominent vitritis and occlusive arteritis; CMV typically less inflammation.
- vs Toxoplasmosis: toxo has focal “headlight in fog” with significant vitritis.

## MANAGEMENT_OVERVIEW
- Urgent systemic + intravitreal antivirals; coordinate with infectious disease.

# DISEASE: PAMM (PARACENTRAL ACUTE MIDDLE MACULOPATHY)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Acute paracentral scotoma with ischemia at the level of intermediate/deep capillary plexus causing middle retinal (INL) involvement.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- OCT: hyperreflective band in INL/OPL acutely; later INL thinning.
- Color fundus: often subtle or grayish lesion.
- OCTA: deep plexus flow impairment.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs AMN: AMN primarily affects outer retina (EZ/ONL) and often has wedge-shaped lesions.
- vs CRAO: CRAO is diffuse inner retinal ischemia.

## ASSOCIATIONS
- RVO, hypotension, vasoconstrictive states.

# DISEASE: AMN (ACUTE MACULAR NEURORETINOPATHY)
## CORE_PHENOTYPE_(ONE-LINER)
- Acute paracentral scotomas (often in young women) with outer retinal ischemic injury.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- OCT: focal disruption at EZ/outer retina and ONL involvement.
- IR imaging: wedge-shaped dark lesions pointing to fovea.
- FAF: subtle changes.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs PAMM: PAMM is middle retinal (INL) band; AMN is outer retinal.
- vs MEWDS: MEWDS has multiple hyperAF dots and wreath FA pattern.

## ASSOCIATIONS
- Viral illness, hypotension, vasoconstrictors.

# DISEASE: SOLAR / PHOTIC RETINOPATHY
## CORE_PHENOTYPE
- Acute photic injury to fovea.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- OCT: focal disruption of EZ and outer retina at fovea.
- Color fundus: small yellowish foveal spot acutely.
- FAF: subtle hyperAF early.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs Macular hole: no full-thickness defect.
- vs AMN: AMN lesions often wedge-shaped and parafoveal.

## MANAGEMENT
- Usually observation.

# DISEASE: OPTIC DISC DRUSEN
## CORE_PHENOTYPE
- Calcified deposits within optic nerve head causing anomalous disc elevation.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- Color fundus: lumpy elevated disc; autofluorescent bodies.
- OCT: hyperreflective buried structures within disc.
- FAF: hyperautofluorescent deposits.
- Ultrasound: highly reflective foci.

## KEY_DISCRIMINATORS
- vs Papilledema: papilledema has disc leakage on FA and no calcification.
- vs Optic neuritis: clinical pain and vision loss pattern differ.

## MANAGEMENT
- Usually observation; monitor visual fields.

# DISEASE: OPTIC DISC PIT WITH MACULOPATHY
## CORE_PHENOTYPE
- Congenital optic disc pit with serous macular detachment.

## TYPICAL_IMAGING
- OCT: schisis-like cavities and serous detachment extending from disc.
- Color fundus: gray pit at optic disc margin.
