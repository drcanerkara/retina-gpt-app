# TORPEDO MACULOPATHY
#tags: dystrophy, atrophy, OCT+FAF, child/young, unilateral, routine

## Core phenotype (one-liner)
- Congenital, torpedo-shaped hypopigmented lesion temporal to the fovea, usually incidental.

## Typical imaging
- Color fundus: well-demarcated hypopigmented torpedo-shaped lesion temporal to fovea.
- OCT: outer retinal attenuation ± focal outer retinal cavitation; RPE alteration.
- FAF: hypoautofluorescent lesion with possible hyperAF rim.

## Key discriminators
- vs CHRPE: CHRPE is flat, deeply pigmented, sharply demarcated.
- vs Choroidal nevus: typically gray, choroidal, not classic torpedo geometry.

# BEST DISEASE (BEST1) / VITELLIFORM MACULAR DYSTROPHY
#tags: dystrophy, vitelliform, OCT+FAF, child/young, bilateral, routine

## Core phenotype (one-liner)
- Inherited vitelliform macular dystrophy with staged subretinal vitelliform material and risk of CNV/atrophy.

## Typical imaging
- Color fundus: “egg-yolk” lesion (vitelliform) or fragmented “scrambled egg” (vitelliruptive).
- OCT: dome-shaped subretinal hyperreflective vitelliform material; later outer retinal disruption/atrophy.
- FAF: strong hyperAF over vitelliform material; hypoAF in atrophic stage.
- OCTA/FA: evaluate for CNV if new fluid/hemorrhage.

## Key discriminators
- vs AOFVD: later onset and often smaller lesions.
- vs CSC: CSC has SRF without dense vitelliform deposit and has pachychoroid context.

# STARGARDT DISEASE (ABCA4) / FUNDUS FLAVIMACULATUS
#tags: dystrophy, bulls-eye, OCT+FAF, child/young, bilateral, routine

## Core phenotype (one-liner)
- Inherited macular dystrophy with flecks and progressive central vision loss; bull’s-eye phenotype may occur.

## Typical imaging
- Color fundus: pisciform flecks ± macular atrophy.
- FAF: flecks (often hyperAF) and macular hypoAF; peripapillary sparing can be suggestive.
- OCT: EZ loss and outer retinal thinning in macula.

## Key discriminators
- vs Hydroxychloroquine toxicity: exposure history and different FAF distribution.
- vs Cone-rod dystrophy: ERG patterns and broader dysfunction.

# CENTRAL SEROUS CHORIORETINOPATHY (CSC)
#tags: pachychoroid, edema, OCT+FA, adult, unilateral, routine

## Core phenotype (one-liner)
- Serous neurosensory detachment due to choroidal hyperpermeability / RPE leak.

## Typical imaging
- OCT: serous retinal detachment ± small PED; pachychoroid features if EDI available.
- FA: focal “inkblot” or “smokestack” leak; late pooling.
- FAF: gravitational tracks in chronic disease.

## Key discriminators
- vs nAMD: older age, drusen, SHRM, hemorrhage favor nAMD.
- vs VKH: VKH has bilateral inflammation and multiple serous detachments with choroidal thickening.

# CENTRAL RETINAL VEIN OCCLUSION (CRVO)
#tags: vascular, occlusion, FA+OCT, elderly, unilateral, urgent

## Core phenotype (one-liner)
- Venous outflow obstruction with dilated tortuous veins and widespread hemorrhages.

## Typical imaging
- Color fundus: diffuse retinal hemorrhages, venous engorgement, disc edema.
- OCT: macular edema common (intraretinal cysts ± SRF).
- FA: delayed venous filling; ischemia assessment.
- OCTA: capillary non-perfusion zones.

## Key discriminators
- vs BRVO: BRVO is sectoral.
- vs OIS: OIS has delayed choroidal filling and carotid disease context.
